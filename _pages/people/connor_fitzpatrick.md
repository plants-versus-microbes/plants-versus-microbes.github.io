    <h1>Connor Fitzpatrick</h1>
    <p class="person-role">Assistant Professor, University of Calgary</p>

    <p>
      Connor completed his undergraduate degree in Plant Science at the University of British Columbia. He then spent several years working as a researcher on a diverse set of projects, including a long-term evolution study in the Caribbean and research on conifer adaptation in the Pacific Northwest, moving between tropical field sites and temperate forests along the way.
    <p>
    He later joined <a href="https://evoecolab.wordpress.com/" target="_blank">Marc Johnson’s lab</a> at the University of Toronto, where he explored the evolutionary ecology of plant–soil feedbacks. He then moved to <a href="https://labs.bio.unc.edu/dangl/" target="_blank">Jeff Dangl’s lab</a> at the University of North Carolina, where he studied how plant microbiomes respond to environmental stress.
    <p>
    In 2026, Connor founded the Plants versus Microbes Lab at the University of Calgary. The lab brings together plant physiology, microbiome biology, and plant pathology to understand how plants interact with their environments and how those interactions shape plant health, crop performance, and disease. Using a range of experimental systems and approaches, the lab aims to connect fundamental discovery with real-world challenges in agriculture, forestry, and beyond.

    </p>

    <h2>Research interests</h2>
